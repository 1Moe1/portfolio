I have acquired and developed a broad and varied range of skills in spatial analysis, mapping, modeling through my work. Works

Prediction of Malaria Hotspots Using GIS/RS, South Khartoum

Mosquito-borne diseases yield a huge burden of disease around the world and lead to about 700 million cases and 1 million deaths from mosquito-borne diseases each year.
The motivation for this is to predict potential mosquito breeding sites to combat malaria in the study area (South Khartoum Locality). The results of the study can be extremely significant in directing efforts for malaria control. It can also make it possible to deploy resources effectively, concentrating on awareness-raising initiatives and evaluating the effectiveness of interventions.

Groundwater Potential Zones Exploration & Analysis Using GIS/RS Techniques

Groundwater is a vital natural resource, and mapping of potential zones can assist in sustainable water resource management.
This project aimed to use GIS and RS to identify groundwater potential zones in Khartoum North. By integrating multiple geospatial datasets and analyzing key indicators, such as land cover, geology, slope, drainage density, lineament density, TWI, NDVI, rainfall, soil, distance to rivers and existing water wells data.
A map that highlights areas with potential for groundwater availability was generated. This kind of maps can contribute to sustainable water resource management and aid in making informed decisions regarding groundwater utilization.

Minerals Exploration Using GIS/RS (Technical Proposal).

As spectral reflectance and signature analysis can help identify variations in rock types and mineral assemblages, the utilization of GIS and RS in the exploration of minerals has gained a lot of popularity lately. This is because it can give important insights into geological features without the need for extensive fieldwork.
The purpose of the study was to investigate how to use GIS and RS to map and assess prospective gold-bearing areas in El-Sherik, Sudan. A detailed evaluation of the potential for gold in the study area can be obtained by combining geological sampling, field observations, and satellite images.

Small Dam Site Selection Using GIS/RS Techniques

Water harvesting is a practice that aims to collect, store, and use runoff or rainwater from various sources for beneficial purposes.
The aim of this study was to locate the best location for a small dam for capturing both rainwater and floodwater. For selecting the best location, a variety of factors, including topography, land cover, rainfall patterns, and hydrological features, were analyzed.
GIS and RS can act as a guide for stakeholders by aiding in planning and management, which can result in improving local water security.

Sudan 2020 Blue Nile & White Nile Floods Analysis

Floods are the most frequent type of disasters. Although they can be predicted, they often cause a massive damage and destruction.
In September 2020, following continous and intense rainfall in North-Eastern Africa, Sudan experienced one of the worst floods in its history. This project aimed to analyze the floods that occurred in Khartoum, specifically in the Blue Nile and White Nile rivers. This project sought to understand these floods, highlight their extent, and evaluate their impact.

California Average Hourly Ozone Concentration 2010-2011.

Ozone buildup is a serious environmental issue that has repercussions for both human health and the long-term viability of the environment.
The motivation of this study was to explore the potential relationship between ozone buildup, household income, and elevation in the state of California. Based on the analysis of data on air quality, elevation, and census tracts with income, it has been found that higher altitude areas frequently have greater ozone concentration levels and that higher income areas also tend to have somewhat higher ozone concentration levels.

Analysis of Flood Prone Areas in Khartoum - Sudan

Nature, infrastructure, and communities are all seriously at risk from floods events. However, by combining geospatial techniques like RS and GIS, early warning systems can be set up to deliver early alerts.
This project's goal was to identify flood-prone zones in Khartoum, which occasionally experiences flooding. For this study, a number of factors were used, including rainfall, elevation, land use, slope, the distance from streams, and historical flood records.This study produced a flood hazard map that indicates areas prone to floods. This map can help in making effective decisions on emergency preparedness.

Analyzing Topographic Map-derived DEMs: A Comparative Study with SRTM Data

Accurate elevation data is crucial for a wide range of applications, from urban planning to environmental analysis. By conducting a comparative analysis, we aim to provide valuable insights into the variations and nuances between these two datasets.
A Comparative Study with SRTM Data aimed to gain valuable insights into the differences in elevation between SRTM DEMs and DEMs generated from contour lines of topographic maps. the study revealed significant differences in elevation between the SRTM DEMs and the topographic map-derived DEMs.

Investigating Agricultural Drought Patterns in Geziera Scheme

For a country like Sudan, food security is significantly threatened by the agricultural drought . Mitigation of this drought requires understanding its causes and effects .
This is an exploratory study that aimed to study agricultural drought patterns in Sudan's Geziera Scheme for the years 2013, 2016, 2019, and 2022. Although the scope of the study was limited, it gave quick insights into the dynamics of drought. This exploratory study also aims to serve as a first step toward a more deep understanding of drought in the Geziera Scheme.

Calculation of Soil Erosion From Basin : Barakat River Basin

Due to the natural water flow and existence of steep slopes, river basins in particular are prone to soil erosion. Controling soil erosion requires in-depth understanding of its causes and impacts.
The aim of this study was to apply the Revised Universal Soil Loss Equation (RUSLE) using some spatial data, including slope, land use, rainfall, and soil properties , to estimate soil erosion in the Barakat River Basin (through 2021).

Urban Heat Islands Mapping

Heat islands are caused by human activity result in higher temperatures in urban areas compared to the surrounding areas.
This study's aim is to learn more about urban heat island phenomena in Dubai.Understanding urban heat islands can assist decision-makers in developing strategies for mitigating the effects of urban heat islands and in creating a more sustainable and livable environment.

Carbon Monoxide (CO): A Quick Study on its Spatiotemporal Distribution in Kenya

The environment and human health are gravely threatened by carbon monoxide (CO) because it is highly poisonous and pollutes the air.
The aim of this study was to find out more about how carbon monoxide (CO) is distributed throughout Kenya between January 1, 2023, and January 31, 2023.In this study, the distribution of CO across Kenya was mapped. This is important for evaluating air quality and detecting locations with high CO levels, which may assist in implementing efficient mitigation strategies.

Exploring Rainfall Anomalies in Sri Lanka

Any deviation from the usual pattern of rainfall can result in a huge impact. Understanding rainfall anomalies can help us understand the spatial and temporal changes in rainfall.
The motive of this study was to find and monitor rainfall anomalies between 2017 and 2019 in Sri Lanka.It provided insightful information and a comprehensive understanding of rainfall anomalies in the study area.

Exploring Waterbodies Turbidity: A Case Study of Lake Albert

Understanding the health and ecological processes of aquatic ecosystems depends heavily on turbidity, which is the measurement of water clarity.
The aim of this study was to look into lake Albert's turbidity. This was accomplished utilizing NDTI, which can offer helpful information for identifying pollution in waterbodies and exploring sediment transport.

LULC Change Detection: Umm Al-Quain

In recent years, land use and land cover (LULC) in the United Arab Emirates has changed significantly. Significant changes in landscape have taken place due to urbanization, development of infrastructure, and economic growth.
This study sought to assess how Umm Al-Quwain's land cover has changed between 2017 and 2022. The results of this study could assist in understanding of the dynamics of land cover changes, providing a foundation for natural resource management and sustainable land use planning.
